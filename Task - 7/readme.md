# 💡 Project Description

Options are financial derivative contracts that provide the buyer with the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price before or on a specified expiration date.

During this task, I studied the concepts presented in the provided learning material and organized the information into a structured presentation covering the fundamentals of options pricing.

The project emphasized understanding financial terminology, pricing concepts, and market behavior rather than software implementation.

---

## Types of Traders

* Buyers
* Sellers (Writers)

---

## Option Premium

Understanding what makes up the price of an option contract.

---

## Intrinsic Value

The value an option would have if exercised immediately.

---

## Time Value

The additional premium traders are willing to pay based on the remaining time until expiration.

---

## Expiration Date

The last date on which an option contract can be exercised.

---

## Strike Price

The predefined price at which the underlying asset can be bought or sold.

---

## Factors Affecting Option Prices

Several factors influence option premiums, including:

* Underlying asset price
* Strike price
* Time to expiration
* Market volatility
* Interest rates
* Dividend announcements

---

## Risk and Reward

The presentation also discusses:

* Potential profits
* Possible losses
* Risk management
* Hedging strategies
